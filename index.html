Interactive Lesson Example

When most people think of corporate training, they aren't thinking of entertaining, impactful, or unique experiences. They are thinking of the same old infodumps that have been around since the early days of Instructional Design.

Engaging lessons aren't about making work fun; they break up the tedium and force learners to shift their focus back to the lesson. I believe that a focused, engaged learner is more likely to understand the skill or concept we are trying to impart to them.

Here is an example of taking something people might find tedious (making an emergency kit) and reimagining it in a more creative way.

I had two objectives: to help the learner understand what should be in an emergency kit and to encourage the learner to actually create an emergency kit.
